I am a Brazilian Computer Science student driven by curiosity, caffeine (specifically iced coffee!), and a healthy dose of restless energy. This curiosity has led me to explore technology across a wide range of fields, from Health and Entertainment to Finance, Sustainability, and Oil & Gas. Because of this, I believe one of my greatest strengths is my multidisciplinarity and my ability to bridge the gap between academic research and real-world industrial applications. 

Some of my work has even caught the media's eye, such as "Onde Está Meu Caramelo?" <a href="https://youtu.be/aRHRn7ZUAMQ" class="btn-link">(Where Is My Puppy?)</a> — a project where I developed a biometric analysis system for lost dogs to help reunite them with their owners, which was featured on Rede Globo, the second-largest TV network in the world. I also founded "Open Synapses," an award-winning initiative recognized by the IEEE, where I bring top AI professionals to the University of Brasília for lectures, aiming to connect students with the industry and showcase different career paths. 

In my free time (which is a bit of a luxury these days), I love diving into stories through books, movies, series, and comics, with a very special place in my heart for Brazilian literature and poetry. If you want to dive deeper into my professional background, feel free to check out my <a href="/cv" class="btn-link">CV</a> tab, or head over to my <a href="/blog" class="btn-link">blog</a> to read some of my latest analyses.
